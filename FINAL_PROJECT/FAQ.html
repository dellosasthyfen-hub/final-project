Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

It's recommended to engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity activity each week, along with muscle-strengthening activities on two or more days a week.

A balanced diet includes a variety of foods from all food groups: fruits, vegetables, grains, protein sources, and dairy. It provides essential nutrients your body needs to function properly.

Setting realistic goals, tracking your progress, finding a workout buddy, and varying your exercise routine can help keep you motivated.

Bodyweight exercises like push-ups, squats, lunges, planks, and burpees are effective home workouts that require no equipment.

Staying hydrated is crucial as it helps regulate body temperature, lubricate joints, and transport nutrients. Drink water before, during, and after exercise.

Adults typically need 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night to support recovery, mental focus, and overall well-being.

Regular stretching, yoga, or Pilates can enhance flexibility. Always warm up before stretching and be consistent for lasting results.

Strength training builds muscle, supports bone health, boosts metabolism, and improves overall strength and posture.

Warm up properly, use correct form, progress gradually, and allow time for recovery. Listening to your body helps avoid overtraining.

Start with simple routines, focus on consistency, and set realistic goals. Combine exercise, nutrition, and rest for balanced progress.
