Nutrition Guides & Meal Ideas

Practical nutrition guidance that supports workouts and everyday health. Find balanced meal templates, quick swaps, and a sample 3-day plan.

Core principles

  • Balance: aim for protein + veg + quality carbs at most meals.
  • Consistency: small changes daily beat large changes occasionally.
  • Practicality: pick meals you enjoy and can prepare regularly.
  • Hydration: water supports performance, digestion, and recovery.

Simple meal templates

Breakfast

Protein (eggs/Greek yogurt) + whole grain (oats/toast) + fruit or veg.

Lunch

Lean protein + mixed salad/veg + a serving of whole grain or starchy veg.

Dinner

Vegetable-forward plate with protein and smaller portion of carbs if weight loss is desired.

Sample 3-day plan (balanced)

Day 1
  • Breakfast: Omelette with spinach + whole grain toast
  • Snack: Banana + handful of almonds
  • Lunch: Grilled chicken salad with mixed greens and quinoa
  • Snack: Carrot sticks + hummus
  • Dinner: Baked salmon, roasted vegetables, small sweet potato
Day 2
  • Breakfast: Greek yogurt parfait with oats and berries
  • Snack: Pear + cottage cheese
  • Lunch: Turkey wrap with salad and avocado
  • Snack: Mixed nuts
  • Dinner: Stir-fry tofu with mixed vegetables and brown rice
Day 3
  • Breakfast: Protein smoothie (milk, banana, protein powder, spinach)
  • Snack: Rice cakes + peanut butter
  • Lunch: Lentil soup + side salad
  • Snack: Greek yogurt
  • Dinner: Grilled lean steak or tempeh, steamed broccoli, quinoa

Quick swaps & snack ideas

  • Swap sugary cereal for oats with fruit and nuts.
  • Choose water or sparkling water over soda.
  • Snack ideas: hummus + veg, apple + nut butter, boiled eggs, Greek yogurt with berries.

Meal prep & shopping tips

  • Plan two batch-cook recipes each week to cover lunches or dinners.
  • Shop the perimeter of the store for whole foods and pick 6-8 versatile ingredients.
  • Pre-portion snacks into small containers to avoid overeating straight from large packages.

Supplements & hydration

  • Most people benefit from a multivitamin and vitamin D if levels are low — check with a clinician.
  • Protein powder is a convenient way to meet protein needs but whole foods are preferred.
  • Hydration: aim for regular water intake; increase around workouts and in hot weather.
