Workouts & Programs

Practical workout plans and sample sessions you can do at home or in the gym. Pick a level, follow the plan, and progress safely.

Warm-up routine

Perform 5-8 minutes of dynamic movements (arm circles, hip swings, leg kicks) and light cardio to increase circulation. Keep intensity low — you should feel warm but not fatigued.

Beginner 4-week program (sample)

Weeks 1-2: Build habit
  • 3 sessions/week: full-body strength (30 minutes) + 1 mobility short flow
  • Focus on technique: squats, push-ups (incline if needed), rows (band/dumbbell), glute bridges, plank
  • Sets/Reps example: 2-3 sets of 8-12 reps per exercise
Weeks 3-4: Increase load
  • 3-4 sessions/week: add one shorter conditioning session (15-20 min intervals)
  • Progress by adding reps, sets, or slightly increasing resistance
  • Include recovery and light cardio (20-30 min walk) on off days

Gym movement: leg press demo

Adjust the seat so your knees track over your feet; avoid locking out at the top. Use a moderate load for controlled reps — 3 sets of 8-12 is a good starting point. Breathe steadily and stop if you feel joint pain.

Sample workouts

Full-body (30 min)
  1. Warm-up: 5 min brisk march or dynamic mobility
  2. 3 rounds: 10 squats, 8 push-ups (knee/incline), 10 bent-over rows (band/dumbbell), 12 glute bridges
  3. Core: 2 x 30s plank
  4. Cool-down: gentle stretching 3-5 min
HIIT (20 min)
  1. Warm-up: 3-4 min mobility
  2. 6 rounds: 30s work / 90s easy — choose from: jumping jacks, mountain climbers, high knees, burpees (modify as needed)
  3. Cool-down: walking and deep breaths 3 min
Mobility flow (10-15 min)

Neck rolls, shoulder circles, cat-cow, thoracic rotations, hip circles, calf stretches — hold each 20-30s. Great post-workout or morning.

Push-up technique

Work with slow, controlled repetitions — 2-4 sets of 6-12 reps depending on strength. Use incline or knee variations if full push-ups are too difficult. Focus on core tension and a straight body line.

Exercise library (quick tips)

Squat

Hips back, chest up, knees tracking toes. Start with bodyweight and progress to weighted variations.

Push-up

Hands under shoulders, maintain a straight line, modify on knees or incline if needed.

Row

Pull through elbows, squeeze shoulder blades together. Use bands or dumbbells for horizontal rows.

Running / jogging

Start with easy efforts: 15-30 minutes at a conversational pace. Keep short, light steps and relaxed shoulders. If new to running, alternate walk/run intervals (e.g., 1-2 min run / 1-2 min walk).

Weekly templates

Balanced week (example)
  • Mon — Strength (full-body)
  • Tue — Mobility + walk
  • Wed — HIIT or conditioning
  • Thu — Strength (upper focus)
  • Fri — Active recovery
  • Sat — Strength (lower focus) or outdoor activity
  • Sun — Rest or gentle mobility

Safety & Progress Tips

  • Warm up and cool down — they reduce injury risk and improve recovery.
  • Progress gradually: increase load by ~5-10% or add 1-2 reps per week.
  • Listen to your body — distinguish between muscle soreness and sharp pain.
  • Track small wins (weights used, reps, how you feel) rather than only the scale.
